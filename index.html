    Introduction to Wolfram Mathematica

    Nov. 13, 10:00 am–Noon • DCL L440

    This workshop targets students who have some background in programming concepts (loops, functions, and variables). (If you know MATLAB, Python, or another language, you'll be fine.) We will conduct a hands-on walkthrough of what Mathematica has to offer and then work through some applications of Mathematica's capabilities.

    We will cover the following topics:

    • Variables, data types, tables, functions
    • Plotting
    • Rules (the core concept of Mathematica)
    • Built-in libraries & technical data
    • Equation and differential equation solution
    • Physical applications

    Notebooks:

    • Whirlwind Tour
    • DNA String (Replacement Rules)
    • Heat Equation (ODE Solution)
    • Pendulum (ODE Solution)
    • Hanging Chain (Special Functions, ODE Solution)
    • Steam Table (Interpolation)
    • Exercises
